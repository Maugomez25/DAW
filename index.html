Robotics project

Robotics in the agriculture are expected to increase to obtain an improvement in crop production by reducing costs in waiting times, laborious and repetitive work, pesticides, water, etc.

State of the art

Objective

For several years, intelligent vehicles capable of navigating autonomously have taken on great importance in research. Nowadays, these robotic vehicles have started to leave the laboratories to occupy more and more places in the world economic life. Mobile robots have been used to perform simple tasks at home such as those of the company i-robot, vehicles capable of delivering articles to our homes, or robots that guide visitors in museums and hotels.
The objective of creating a robot for the agriculture is to free the farmer from some tasks such as sowing, watering or harvesting, and allowing him to perform other tasks focused on administrative, commercial or scientific issues. (Agricultura Moderna, 2017)

Aplications

  • Sowing the seed
    • Pros
      • An easy way to make sowing.
      • Optimizing repetitive hard work.
      • Exhaustive work can be done without any human efforts.
    • Cons
      • The battery could last not as much.
      • The container may be too small.
      • The road could be a challenge.

  • Robot fruit picker
    • Pros
      • It can be applied to multiple crops.
      • The robot can work throughout the day under the sun.
      • It deals with a repetitive work (collecting fruit).
      • The robot is capable of carrying a lot of weight.
    • Cons
      • The mechanism to get the fruit must be accurate and strong.
      • It incorporates a complex vision system.
      • The robot requires big batteries and a big power source.

  • Soil sampling Robot
    • Pros
      • Can cover big land areas for more accurate sampling.
      • A better random factor leads to a better mineral.
      • Exhaustive work can be done without any human efforts.
      • Can use solar power to feed its batteries.
    • Cons
      • Picking tools must be clean before each sampling.
      • Must have enough room to carry all the samples.
      • Requires long lasting batteries.
